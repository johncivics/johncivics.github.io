Cancer

The Unequal Treatment of Cancer Based on Socioeconomic Status

Background Perspectives Actions Taken Solutions Take Action

Background

    The treatment of cancer in Canada is based on wealth and socioeconomic status, allowing wealthy people to afford better medical equipment and to skip the line. People in poorer communities will get less access to cancer treatment and thus are more likely to die from cancer. This is concerning because 44% of Canadians will contract cancer during their lifetime, and cancer develops more frequently in poorer communities. Additionally, poorer communities usually lack doctors and treatment options. The mortality rate for a 5-year cancer is 27% in urban areas, but it is way higher at 39% in poorer areas. The government is unable to act due to economic issues, as taxes will have to be increased for this to happen. This NGO, Canadian Cancer Society, relies on mostly donations, sponsorships, and government grants. Although they can provide free medical equipment and treatment, they will not be able to solve the long-term problem.

Perspectives and Power

    As the wage gaps increase, the more people are affected by this socioeconomic class difference. The people who are affected by this issue vastly outnumber the people who do not get affected. Political parties would advocate for the lower classes to gain influence. For example, if the lower class demand more equality, the parties should try their best to give them more equality because of electoral pressure. Human rights and equality is involved in this as everyone deserves to be treated the same, especially with a life threatening disease. The wealthy can afford to pay people to skip the line, while the poor lack medical equipment.
    The Liberals want to impose fines to people who skip the line, while the People's Party will hire more doctors and invest into more hospitals.

Actions Taken

    Instead of providing more free treatment, the political parties are trying to make their services cheaper by hiring more doctors and generally investing more into the healthcare sector. However, The Canadian Cancer Society is working to provide free medical equipment and caregivers to cancer patients.
    ‎
    Liberal Party:
    - Fines wealthy people for trying to skip the healthcare line
    ‎
    People's Party:
    - Let provincial and territorial governments set up their own private and public healthcare systems
    ‎
    The Canadian Cancer Society NGO:
    - Provides cancer treatment, medical equipment, and caregivers to people who cannot afford it
    - Relies on donations, sponsorships, and government grants
    - Allocates some money to research that solves cancer.

Solutions

    Liberal Party's Solution:
    Fines the wealthy people who are trying to pay to skip the lines, however this only delays the problem
    Governments and political parties have the spending power and investment to try to make curing cancer and healthcare in general cheaper, faster, and more accessible (hiring doctors)
    ‎
    Solution 1:
    Build more hospitals and invest more into the medical field, hiring more doctors which makes healthcare cheaper and more accessible, especially to non-urban and poorer areas lacking doctors.
    ‎
    Solution 2:
    Combine the Canadian Cancer Society into the government, with citizen donations still being accepted for the NGO
    This allows the NGO to act alongside the government, taking a cut of the taxes and using the increased funding to invest more into doctors, research, and hospitals to have a long-term impact
